Time and Work (LCM Method)

Core Concept: The LCM Method

Question

What is the most efficient way to solve Time and Work problems without using fractions?

Answer

By using the LCM (Least Common Multiple) of the days given to represent the "Total Work," we can calculate individual "Efficiency" (work done per day) in whole numbers.

Explanation

The speaker outlines a three-step process:

  1. Find Total Work: Take the LCM of the number of days each person takes to complete the work. This LCM value becomes the total units of work.
  2. Find Efficiency: Efficiency is the work done per day. The formula is:
    Efficiency = Total Work / Time Taken
  3. Calculate Time: To find the time taken to complete the work (either individually or together), use the formula:
    Time = Total Work / Combined Efficiency

Important Notes: The speaker emphasizes, "Forget the old method of using fractions like 1/10 and 1/15. It's slow and confusing. The LCM method is the best trick. The LCM gives you a nice, clean number for the total work, and the efficiencies become simple integers. This makes calculations much faster and easier."

Question 1: Working Together

Question

A can do a piece of work in 10 days and B can do it in 15 days. In how many days will both of them do it together?

Answer

6 days.

Explanation

  1. Total Work: LCM of 10 and 15 is 30 units.
  2. Efficiency of A: Total Work / Time = 30 / 10 = 3 units/day.
  3. Efficiency of B: Total Work / Time = 30 / 15 = 2 units/day.
  4. Combined Efficiency (A+B): 3 + 2 = 5 units/day.
  5. Time Taken Together: Total Work / Combined Efficiency = 30 / 5 = 6 days.

Question 2: Three People Working Together

Question

A, B, and C can complete a piece of work in 12, 15, and 20 days respectively. In how many days can they together complete the work?

Answer

5 days.

Explanation

  1. Total Work: LCM of 12, 15, and 20 is 60 units.
  2. Efficiency of A: 60 / 12 = 5 units/day.
  3. Efficiency of B: 60 / 15 = 4 units/day.
  4. Efficiency of C: 60 / 20 = 3 units/day.
  5. Combined Efficiency (A+B+C): 5 + 4 + 3 = 12 units/day.
  6. Time Taken Together: Total Work / Combined Efficiency = 60 / 12 = 5 days.

Question 3: Working in Pairs

Question

A and B can do a work in 12 days, B and C in 15 days, and C and A in 20 days. If A, B, and C work together, they will complete the work in how many days?

Answer

10 days.

Explanation

  1. Total Work: LCM of 12, 15, and 20 is 60 units.
  2. Efficiency of (A+B): 60 / 12 = 5 units/day.
  3. Efficiency of (B+C): 60 / 15 = 4 units/day.
  4. Efficiency of (C+A): 60 / 20 = 3 units/day.
  5. Add all efficiencies: (A+B) + (B+C) + (C+A) = 5 + 4 + 3 = 12 units/day.
  6. This simplifies to 2A + 2B + 2C = 12, or 2(A+B+C) = 12.
  7. Combined Efficiency (A+B+C): 12 / 2 = 6 units/day.
  8. Time Taken Together: Total Work / Combined Efficiency = 60 / 6 = 10 days.

Important Notes: The speaker points out the critical step: "When you add the efficiencies of pairs, you get the efficiency of 2A, 2B, and 2C because each person is counted twice. You MUST divide this sum by 2 to find the combined efficiency of just A, B, and C. Forgetting to divide by 2 is the most common mistake in this type of problem."

Question 4: Work Done and Remaining Work

Question

A can do a piece of work in 25 days and B in 20 days. They work together for 5 days and then A leaves. In how many days will B finish the remaining work?

Answer

11 days.

Explanation

  1. Total Work: LCM of 25 and 20 is 100 units.
  2. Efficiency of A: 100 / 25 = 4 units/day.
  3. Efficiency of B: 100 / 20 = 5 units/day.
  4. Combined Efficiency (A+B): 4 + 5 = 9 units/day.
  5. Work done in 5 days together: 9 units/day * 5 days = 45 units.
  6. Remaining Work: Total Work - Work Done = 100 - 45 = 55 units.
  7. Time for B to finish remaining work: Remaining Work / B's Efficiency = 55 / 5 = 11 days.
