CLOZE TEST: STRATEGIES & PRACTICE

  • Definition & Concept
  • Tricks to Solve
  • 100 Solved Examples

Definition & Concept

Concept: A Cloze Test is an exercise where a passage is provided with certain words removed (blanks). Students are given a set of options for each blank and must choose the most appropriate word to fill it.

Why is it important for competitive exams?

The Cloze Test is a powerful tool used by examiners because it tests multiple English language skills simultaneously:

  • Reading Comprehension: You must understand the overall meaning and tone of the passage.
  • Grammar: Your choice must be grammatically correct (e.g., right tense, preposition, subject-verb agreement).
  • Vocabulary: You must know the precise meaning of words and how they fit into the context.
  • Collocation: You need to recognize which words naturally go together.

Tricks and Strategies to Solve Cloze Tests

A systematic approach is far more effective than just guessing. Follow these steps to improve your accuracy.

A Step-by-Step Guide

  1. 1. Read the Entire Passage First: Before looking at the options, read the whole passage quickly. This helps you understand the main idea, the overall tone (positive, negative, narrative, analytical), and the tense (past, present, future).
  2. 2. Analyze the Blank: Look at the words immediately before and after the blank. This immediate context often provides strong grammatical or logical clues.
  3. 3. Apply Grammar Rules:
    • Check for **Subject-Verb Agreement**: Does the subject need a singular or plural verb?
    • Identify the correct **Tense**: Is the passage in the past, present, or future?
    • Find the right **Preposition**: Words like `of`, `for`, `in`, `on` are often tested.
    • Look for **Conjunctions** that create a logical link (e.g., `but`, `although`, `therefore`).
    • Check for **Articles** (`a`, `an`, `the`) and other determiners.
  4. 4. Use Vocabulary and Collocation: If grammar doesn't provide a clear answer, the blank is likely testing vocabulary.
    • Think about the **meaning** of the options in the context.
    • Look for **collocations** (words that naturally go together), e.g., "make a mistake," not "do a mistake."
  5. 5. Use the Elimination Technique: Actively eliminate options that are clearly wrong. This increases your chances of selecting the correct answer, even if you are not 100% sure.
  6. 6. Re-read the Sentence: After you have chosen an answer, read the complete sentence with your chosen word to ensure it sounds logical and grammatically correct.
