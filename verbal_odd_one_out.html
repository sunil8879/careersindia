Odd One Out

Question 1

QUESTION: Find the odd one out from the following options:

  • January
  • May
  • July
  • November

ANSWER: November

EXPLANATION: The logic is based on the number of days in each month. January, May, and July each have 31 days. November is the only option with 30 days.

Question 2

QUESTION: Find the odd one out from the following options:

  • Apple
  • Mango
  • Potato
  • Watermelon

ANSWER: Potato

EXPLANATION: All options are food items. However, Apple, Mango, and Watermelon are fruits. Potato is a vegetable (a tuber that grows underground).

Question 3

QUESTION: Find the odd one out from the following options:

  • Tiger
  • Lion
  • Leopard
  • Cow

ANSWER: Cow

EXPLANATION: Tiger, Lion, and Leopard are all wild, carnivorous animals. The Cow is a domestic, herbivorous animal.

Question 4

QUESTION: Find the odd one out from the following options:

  • Teacher
  • Professor
  • Student
  • Headmaster

ANSWER: Student

EXPLANATION: Teacher, Professor, and Headmaster are all roles involved in teaching or administration within an educational institution (staff/faculty). The Student is the one who learns or receives education.

Question 5

QUESTION: Find the odd one out from the following options:

  • River
  • Lake
  • Pond
  • Pool

ANSWER: Pool

EXPLANATION: River, Lake, and Pond are all natural bodies of water. A Pool (specifically a swimming pool) is an artificial, man-made structure.

Question 6

QUESTION: Find the odd one out from the following options:

  • Rose
  • Lotus
  • Marigold
  • Lily

ANSWER: Lotus

EXPLANATION: The classification is based on where the flowers grow. Rose, Marigold, and Lily are flowers that primarily grow on land. The Lotus is an aquatic flower that grows in water.

Question 7

QUESTION: Find the odd one out from the following options:

  • Car
  • Bus
  • Scooter
  • Aeroplane

ANSWER: Aeroplane

EXPLANATION: All options are modes of transport. Car, Bus, and Scooter are all modes of land transport. The Aeroplane is the only mode of air transport.
