Counting Figures

What is Figure Counting?

Figure Counting problems require you to find the total number of a specific shape (like triangles, squares, or straight lines) within a more complex, composite figure. These questions test your ability to observe systematically and apply shortcuts or formulas.

General Strategy: The Systematic Approach

  1. Identify the Smallest Units: Start by counting the most basic, individual shapes.
  2. Count Composite Shapes: Systematically look for larger shapes formed by combining the smaller units.
  3. Use Labels: For very complex figures, label the vertices or regions and list the combinations.
  4. Look for Formulas and Shortcuts: Many counting problems use standardized patterns. Learning the formulas is key.

1. Counting Straight Lines

The best method is to count in three groups and add them up.

Total Lines = (Horizontal Lines) + (Vertical Lines) + (Slanting/Diagonal Lines)

2. Counting Triangles

A common pattern involves a triangle with its base divided into multiple parts.

If a triangle's base is divided into 'n' parts, Total Triangles = n * (n + 1) / 2

3. Counting Squares

For grid-based problems, there are standard formulas:

  • For an n x n grid (e.g., 3x3):
    Total Squares = 1² + 2² + ... + n²
  • For an m x n grid (e.g., 4x3):
    Total Squares = (m*n) + (m-1)*(n-1) + ... (continue until a term becomes zero).
