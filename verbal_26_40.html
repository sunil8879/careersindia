DIRECTION SENSE TEST

Theory: Introduction

INTRODUCTION

Directions Sense Test Questions are based on three principles:

  1. Calculation of Distance
  2. Finding the Direction
  3. Finding both the Distance and the Direction

You can be asked to calculate the distance or the direction. The first thing to be done is to draw the basic direction map with the indication of all the directions. Based on the direction map, you can quickly answer the direction sense test verbal reasoning questions.

SOME IMPORTANT TERMS

DISPLACEMENT: It is the shortest distance between the starting and the finishing point.

DISTANCE: It is the actual length of path travelled by an object or person.

DIRECTION: It is the measurement of position of one thing with respect to another.

There are two types of directions:

  1. PRIME DIRECTIONS or MAIN DIRECTIONS: Based on the movement of the sun, they are of four types – North, South, East and West.
  2. CARDINAL DIRECTIONS: The directions between the Prime Directions are known as Cardinal directions. They are of four types.

TYPE 2 - BASED ON DIRECTION

Question: Example 1

I am facing North. I turn 100º in the clockwise direction and then 145º in the anti-clockwise direction. Which direction am I facing now?

  1. North-East
  2. North-West
  3. West
  4. South
Diagram: Solution to Example 1
Page 27 of 309
Explanation: Solution to Example 1

The net rotation is 145° (anti-clockwise) - 100° (clockwise) = 45° anti-clockwise from North. A 45° anti-clockwise turn from North leads to the North-West direction.

Correct Answer: (b) North-West

Question: Example 2

A person walks towards his house at 8:00 am and observes his shadow to his right. In which direction is he walking?

Source: (SSC (CPO) 2014)

Explanation: Solution to Example 2

At 8 am the sun is in the East. The shadow is cast in the West. If the person's shadow is to his right, and his right is the West, he must be facing South.

Aman walked 20 m South, turned left, walked 25 m, turned left, walked 20 m, turned right, walked 15 m. At what distance is he from the starting point and in which direction?

  1. 40 m, West

  4. 45 m, East

Explanation: Solution to Example 1

Aman starts at A, goes south 20m to B, left (east) 25m to C, left (north) 20m to D, and right (east) 15m to E. The 20m south and 20m north movements cancel each other out vertically. Horizontally, he moves 25m east and then another 15m east. His final position E is 25 + 15 = 40m to the East of his starting point A.

Correct Answer: (b) 40 m, East
