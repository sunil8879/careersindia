Government Job Examinations Overview

Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) Examinations

Important: Always refer to the official UPSC notification for the specific year's exam for the most accurate and detailed information. Syllabus details provided here are broad overviews. Salaries are indicative and subject to change and allowances.

1. UPSC Civil Services Examination (CSE)

Posts/Services Offered (Illustrative):

  • All India Services:
    • Indian Administrative Service (IAS)
    • Indian Police Service (IPS)
    • Indian Forest Service (IFoS) - (Separate Mains & Interview)
  • Central Services (Group A):
    • Indian Foreign Service (IFS)
    • Indian Audit and Accounts Service (IA&AS)
    • Indian Revenue Service (IRS - Income Tax)
    • Indian Revenue Service (IRS - Customs & Indirect Taxes)
    • Indian Defence Accounts Service (IDAS)
    • Indian Railway Traffic Service (IRTS)
    • Indian Railway Accounts Service (IRAS)
    • Indian Railway Personnel Service (IRPS)
    • Indian Postal Service (IPoS)
    • Indian Civil Accounts Service (ICAS)
    • Indian Information Service (IIS)
    • Indian Trade Service (ITS)
    • Indian Corporate Law Service (ICLS)
  • Central Services (Group B):
    • Armed Forces Headquarters Civil Service
    • Delhi, Andaman & Nicobar Islands, Lakshadweep, Daman & Diu and Dadra & Nagar Haveli Civil Service (DANICS)
    • Delhi, Andaman & Nicobar Islands, Lakshadweep, Daman & Diu and Dadra & Nagar Haveli Police Service (DANIPS)
    • Pondicherry Civil Service
    • Pondicherry Police Service

Eligibility Criteria (General):

  • Nationality: Citizen of India (specific variations for some services).
  • Age Limit: 21 to 32 years (relaxations for SC/ST, OBC, PwBD, etc.).
  • Educational Qualification: A degree from any recognized university or equivalent.

Syllabus (Broad Overview):

  • Stage I: Preliminary Examination (Objective Type)
    • Paper I: General Studies (GS) - Current events, History of India and Indian National Movement, Indian and World Geography, Indian Polity and Governance, Economic and Social Development, General issues on Environmental Ecology, Bio-diversity and Climate Change, General Science.
    • Paper II: Civil Services Aptitude Test (CSAT) - Comprehension, Interpersonal skills including communication skills, Logical reasoning and analytical ability, Decision making and problem-solving, General mental ability, Basic numeracy (Class X level), Data interpretation (Class X level). (Qualifying with 33% marks)
  • Stage II: Main Examination (Written - Descriptive Type)
    • Qualifying Papers:
      • Paper A: One Indian Language (from 8th Schedule)
      • Paper B: English
    • Papers for Merit:
      • Paper I: Essay
      • Paper II: General Studies I (Indian Heritage and Culture, History and Geography of the World and Society)
      • Paper III: General Studies II (Governance, Constitution, Polity, Social Justice and International relations)
      • Paper IV: General Studies III (Technology, Economic Development, Bio-diversity, Environment, Security and Disaster Management)
      • Paper V: General Studies IV (Ethics, Integrity, and Aptitude)
      • Paper VI: Optional Subject – Paper 1
      • Paper VII: Optional Subject – Paper 2
  • Stage III: Personality Test (Interview)
    • Assesses mental alertness, critical powers of assimilation, clear and logical exposition, balance of judgment, variety and depth of interest, ability for social cohesion and leadership, intellectual and moral integrity.

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • IAS: Policy formulation & implementation, district administration, development work, managing public sector undertakings.
  • IPS: Maintaining law and order, crime prevention and detection, managing police forces, intelligence gathering.
  • IFS (Foreign): Representing India abroad, managing diplomatic missions, negotiating international agreements, promoting India's interests.
  • IRS (IT/C&IT): Tax administration (direct/indirect), policy formulation related to taxation, investigation of tax evasion.
  • Others: Vary widely depending on the service.

Salary (Indicative Starting):

  • Most Group A services start at Pay Level 10 of the 7th Pay Commission (Basic Pay: ₹56,100).
  • Gross salary (including DA, HRA, TA) can be around ₹80,000 - ₹1,00,000+ per month initially.

2. UPSC Indian Forest Service (IFoS) Examination

Posts/Services Offered:

  • Indian Forest Service (IFoS) - An All India Service.

Eligibility Criteria (General):

  • Nationality: Citizen of India.
  • Age Limit: 21 to 32 years (relaxations apply).
  • Educational Qualification: Bachelor's degree with at least one of the subjects namely Animal Husbandry & Veterinary Science, Botany, Chemistry, Geology, Mathematics, Physics, Statistics and Zoology OR a Bachelor's degree in Agriculture, Forestry or in Engineering from a recognised University or equivalent.
  • Physical Standards: Specific physical fitness standards are required.

Syllabus (Broad Overview):

  • Stage I: Preliminary Examination (Common with CSE Prelims).
  • Stage II: Main Examination (Written - Descriptive Type)
    • Paper I: General English (300 marks)
    • Paper II: General Knowledge (300 marks)
    • Papers III, IV, V, VI: Any two optional subjects to be selected from the list (each subject has two papers, 200 marks each from a prescribed list).
  • Stage III: Personality Test (Interview) (300 marks) + Walking Test.

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • Protection, conservation, and management of forests and wildlife; policy implementation.

Salary (Indicative Starting):

  • Starts at Pay Level 10 (Basic Pay: ₹56,100).

3. UPSC Engineering Services Examination (ESE / IES)

Posts/Services Offered:

  • Group 'A' Services/Posts in Civil, Mechanical, Electrical, Electronics & Telecommunication Engineering in organizations like Railways, CPWD, MES, etc.

Eligibility Criteria (General):

  • Nationality: Citizen of India.
  • Age Limit: 21 to 30 years (relaxations apply).
  • Educational Qualification: Degree in relevant Engineering discipline.

Syllabus (Broad Overview):

  • Stage I: Preliminary Examination (Objective Type)
    • Paper I: General Studies and Engineering Aptitude (Common).
    • Paper II: Engineering Discipline Specific.
  • Stage II: Main Examination (Descriptive Type)
    • Paper I: Engineering Discipline Specific.
    • Paper II: Engineering Discipline Specific.
  • Stage III: Personality Test (Interview)

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • Technical & managerial roles in government engineering projects, infrastructure development.

Salary (Indicative Starting):

  • Starts at Pay Level 10 (Basic Pay: ₹56,100).

4. UPSC Combined Defence Services (CDS) Examination

Posts/Services Offered:

  • Officer cadre entry to Indian Military Academy (IMA), Indian Naval Academy (INA), Air Force Academy (AFA), Officers' Training Academy (OTA).

Eligibility Criteria (General):

  • Nationality: Unmarried Male/Female (OTA only for Female) citizens of India.
  • Age Limit: IMA/INA: 19-24 years; AFA: 20-24 years; OTA: 19-25 years.
  • Educational Qualification: Degree (IMA/OTA); Engg. Degree (INA); Degree with Phy/Maths at 10+2 or BE (AFA).
  • Physical Standards: Mandatory.

Syllabus (Broad Overview):

  • Stage I: Written Exam - English, General Knowledge, Elementary Mathematics (IMA/INA/AFA); English, GK (OTA).
  • Stage II: SSB Interview (Multi-day psychological & group tests).

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • Leadership in Army, Navy, Air Force; commanding troops, strategic operations.

Salary (Indicative Starting for Lieutenant):

  • Pay Level 10 (Basic Pay: ₹56,100) + Military Service Pay (MSP) + allowances.

5. UPSC National Defence Academy (NDA) & Naval Academy (NA) Examination

Posts/Services Offered:

  • Entry to Army, Navy, Air Force wings of NDA & 10+2 Cadet Entry at INA.

Eligibility Criteria (General):

  • Nationality: Unmarried Male/Female citizens of India.
  • Age Limit: 16.5 to 19.5 years.
  • Educational Qualification: 12th Class pass (Army Wing); 12th Class pass with Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics (Air Force and Naval Wings of NDA and for 10+2 Cadet Entry Scheme at INA).
  • Physical Standards: Mandatory.

Syllabus (Broad Overview):

  • Stage I: Written Examination
    • Mathematics (Algebra, Matrices & Determinants, Trigonometry, Analytical Geometry, Calculus, Vector Algebra, Statistics & Probability).
    • General Ability Test (GAT - English & General Knowledge: Physics, Chemistry, Gen. Science, History, Geography, Current Events).
  • Stage II: Interview by Services Selection Board (SSB).

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • Rigorous academic and military training at NDA/INA, then commissioned as officers.

Salary (During Training & After Commissioning):

  • Cadets receive a stipend. Upon commissioning as Lieutenant, salary as per Pay Level 10 + MSP.

6. UPSC Combined Medical Services (CMS) Examination

Posts/Services Offered:

  • Medical Officers in Railways, Delhi Municipalities, Central Health Service, etc.

Eligibility Criteria (General):

  • Nationality: Citizen of India.
  • Age Limit: Below 32 years (relaxations apply).
  • Educational Qualification: Final M.B.B.S. (written & practical) passed.

Syllabus (Broad Overview):

  • Part I: Written Exam (Objective - CBT) - Paper I (General Medicine & Paediatrics), Paper II (Surgery, Gynae & Obs, PSM).
  • Part II: Personality Test (Interview).

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • Medical care in govt hospitals/dispensaries, public health programs.

Salary (Indicative Starting):

  • Pay Level 10 (Basic: ₹56,100) + NPA + allowances.

7. UPSC Indian Economic Service (IES) / Indian Statistical Service (ISS) Examination

Posts/Services Offered:

  • IES (Group 'A'), ISS (Group 'A').

Eligibility Criteria (General):

  • Nationality: Citizen of India.
  • Age Limit: 21 to 30 years (relaxations apply).
  • Educational Qualification:
    • IES: Post-Graduate Degree in Economics/Applied Economics/Business Economics/Econometrics.
    • ISS: Bachelor's Degree with Statistics/Mathematical Statistics/Applied Statistics as a subject OR Master's degree in Statistics/Mathematical Statistics/Applied Statistics.

Syllabus (Broad Overview):

  • Part I: Written Examination (Descriptive Type)
    • IES: General English, GS, General Economics I, II, III, Indian Economics.
    • ISS: General English, GS, Statistics I & II (Objective), Statistics III & IV (Descriptive).
  • Part II: Viva Voce (Interview).

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • IES: Economic planning, policy formulation, analysis.
  • ISS: Statistical data collection, analysis, interpretation for policy.

Salary (Indicative Starting):

  • Pay Level 10 (Basic: ₹56,100).

Staff Selection Commission (SSC) Examinations

Important: For SSC Exams, always refer to the official SSC notification for details. Pay Levels mentioned are for basic pay and do not include allowances.

1. SSC Combined Graduate Level (CGL) Examination

Posts Recruited For (Illustrative):

  • Assistant Audit Officer (AAO), Assistant Accounts Officer (AAO)
  • Assistant Section Officer (CSS, MEA, Railways, etc.)
  • Inspector of Income Tax, Inspector (Central Excise), Inspector (Preventive Officer), Inspector (Examiner)
  • Assistant Enforcement Officer, Sub-Inspector (CBI, NIA)
  • Auditor (CAG, CGDA, CGA), Accountant/Junior Accountant
  • Senior Secretariat Assistant/Upper Division Clerk, Tax Assistant, Statistical Investigator Gr. II

Eligibility Criteria (General):

  • Age Limit: Varies (18-30/20-30/up to 32 yrs), relaxations apply.
  • Educational Qualification: Bachelor's Degree. Specifics for AAO, JSO.

Syllabus (Broad Overview):

  • Tier-I (Computer Based Examination - Objective): General Intelligence & Reasoning, General Awareness, Quantitative Aptitude, English Comprehension.
  • Tier-II (Computer Based Examination - Objective): Mathematical Abilities, Reasoning & GI, English, General Awareness, Computer Knowledge (Qualifying), Statistics (JSO), GS-Finance/Economics (AAO).
  • Skill Test (DEST/CPT) if applicable.

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • Assistant roles (file work, drafting), Inspector roles (field work, investigation), Auditor/Accountant roles.

Salary (Indicative Pay Levels):

  • Range from Pay Level 4 (₹25,500) to Pay Level 8 (₹47,600) basic.

2. SSC Combined Higher Secondary Level (CHSL) Examination

Posts Recruited For:

  • LDC/JSA, Postal Assistant (PA)/Sorting Assistant (SA), Data Entry Operator (DEO).

Eligibility Criteria (General):

  • Age Limit: 18-27 years (relaxations apply).

Syllabus (Broad Overview):

  • Tier-I (CBE - Objective): English, General Intelligence, Quantitative Aptitude, General Awareness.

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • Clerical work, data entry, postal operations.

Salary (Indicative Pay Levels):

  • LDC/JSA: Pay Level 2 (₹19,900 basic).
  • PA/SA/DEO: Pay Level 4 (₹25,500 basic).

Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) Examinations

Important: For RRB Exams, refer to official RRB CENs. Pay Levels do not include significant allowances like Running Allowance for ALPs/Guards.

1. RRB Non-Technical Popular Categories (NTPC) Examination

Posts Recruited For:

  • Undergraduate Posts (12th Pass): Junior Clerk cum Typist, Accounts Clerk cum Typist, Junior Time Keeper, Trains Clerk, Commercial cum Ticket Clerk.
  • Graduate Posts (Bachelor's Degree): Goods Guard, Senior Commercial cum Ticket Clerk, Senior Clerk cum Typist, Junior Account Assistant cum Typist, Senior Time Keeper, Commercial Apprentice, Station Master.

Eligibility Criteria (General):

  • Nationality: Citizen of India.
  • Age Limit: UG: 18-30 yrs; Grad: 18-33 yrs (relaxations apply).
  • Educational Qualification: 12th Pass (UG posts); Bachelor's Degree (Graduate posts). Typing proficiency for some.

Syllabus (Broad Overview - Multi-Stage):

  • 1st Stage CBT (CBT-1) (Screening): Mathematics, General Intelligence & Reasoning, General Awareness.
  • 2nd Stage CBT (CBT-2) (Post group specific): Mathematics, General Intelligence & Reasoning, General Awareness (in-depth).
  • Computer Based Aptitude Test (CBAT) (Station Master & Traffic Assistant - Qualifying).
  • Typing Skill Test (TST) (Clerk, JAA, etc. - Qualifying).
  • Document Verification & Medical Examination.

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • Clerical, Accounts, Commercial (Ticket/Goods operations), Station Management.

Salary (Indicative Pay Levels - 7th CPC):

  • Undergraduate Posts: Pay Level 2 (₹19,900 basic) to Level 3 (₹21,700 basic).
  • Graduate Posts: Pay Level 5 (₹29,200 basic) to Level 6 (₹35,400 basic).

2. RRB Assistant Loco Pilot (ALP) & Technicians Examination

Posts Recruited For:

  • Assistant Loco Pilot (ALP), Various Technician posts (Fitter, Electrician, etc.).

Eligibility Criteria (General):

  • Nationality: Citizen of India.
  • Age Limit: 18-28 or 18-30 years (varies, relaxations apply).
  • Educational Qualification:
    • ALP: Matriculation + ITI / Diploma / Degree in Engg. (specific trades).
    • Technicians: Matriculation + ITI / Act Apprenticeship (specific trades). Some 10+2 PCM.

Syllabus (Broad Overview - Multi-Stage):

  • First Stage CBT (CBT-1) (Screening): Mathematics, General Intelligence & Reasoning, General Science, General Awareness on Current Affairs.
  • Second Stage CBT (CBT-2):
    • Part A: Mathematics, GI & Reasoning, Basic Science & Engineering, GA.
    • Part B (Qualifying): Relevant Trade Syllabus.
  • Computer Based Aptitude Test (CBAT) (Only for ALP - Qualifying).
  • Document Verification & Medical Examination (Strict for ALP).

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • ALP: Assisting Loco Pilots in train operations.
  • Technicians: Maintenance & repair of railway assets.

Salary (Indicative Pay Levels - 7th CPC):

  • ALP/Technician Grade III: Pay Level 2 (₹19,900 basic). ALP gets Running Allowance.

3. RRB Junior Engineer (JE) Examination

Posts Recruited For:

  • Junior Engineer (JE - Civil, Electrical, Mechanical, S&T, etc.), JE (IT), Depot Material Superintendent (DMS), Chemical & Metallurgical Assistant (CMA).

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • Supervision of technical work, maintenance, project execution in respective Engg. depts, IT, Stores, Labs.

Salary (Indicative Pay Levels - 7th CPC):

  • All posts generally Pay Level 6 (₹35,400 basic).

State Public Service Commission (PSC) Examinations

Crucial Note: Each state in India has its own Public Service Commission (e.g., UPPSC, MPSC, TNPSC, RPSC, KPSC, BPSC, WBPSC, etc.). The exam patterns, syllabus, eligibility criteria, and pay scales vary significantly from state to state. Always refer to the official notification released by the specific State PSC for the examination you are interested in. The information below provides a general idea.

State PSCs conduct exams for various Group A, B, and sometimes C posts in the administrative, police, judicial, forest, and other departments of the respective state governments.

1. State Civil Services Exams (e.g., PCS, RAS, KAS, WBCS, etc.)

Posts Recruited For (Illustrative - Varies by State):

  • Deputy Collector / Sub Divisional Magistrate (SDM) / Revenue Divisional Officer (RDO)
  • Deputy Superintendent of Police (DSP) / Assistant Commissioner of Police (ACP)
  • Tehsildar / Mamlatdar / Assistant Commissioner (Revenue)
  • Block Development Officer (BDO) / Taluk Development Officer
  • Various other Class I & Class II state-level administrative and executive posts.

Eligibility Criteria (General - Varies by State):

  • Nationality: Citizen of India. Domicile/local language knowledge often required/preferred.
  • Age Limit: Generally 21 to 30/32/35/40 years (varies widely, relaxations apply).
  • Educational Qualification: A Bachelor's Degree from a recognized University.

Syllabus (Broad Overview - Typically Multi-Stage, Varies by State):

  • Preliminary Examination (Objective - Screening): Paper I: General Studies (India & State focus), Paper II: General Aptitude / CSAT (often qualifying).
  • Main Examination (Written - Descriptive): General Hindi/State Language, General English, Essay, Multiple GS Papers (India & State GK, History, Geography, Polity, Economy, Ethics), Optional Subjects (some states).
  • Interview / Personality Test.

Scope of Work/Nature of Job:

  • Implementation of state policies, district/sub-district administration, law & order, revenue, development.

Salary (Indicative - Varies Greatly by State):

  • SDM/DSP often start at State Pay Level 10 to 14 equivalent (basic ₹50k-₹70k range).

Other Notable Government Recruitments (Overview)

ISRO Recruitment Exams

ISRO conducts recruitment for Scientists/Engineers (typically B.E./B.Tech/M.Sc./Ph.D. in relevant disciplines, selection often via written test & interview, or GATE scores for some), Technicians, Draughtsmen, Technical Assistants (Diploma/ITI), and Administrative/Accounts/Purchase & Stores Officers, Assistants, Junior Personal Assistants. Eligibility and syllabus are highly specific to the post advertised. Refer to ISRO's official career portal. Salaries are as per GoI scales with good allowances.

DRDO Recruitment Exams

DRDO recruits Scientists (Group A Gazetted, via Recruitment & Assessment Centre - RAC, typically M.E/M.Tech/Ph.D.), and Technical Staff (CEPTAM exam for Senior Technical Assistant-B [STA-B - Diploma/B.Sc.] & Technician-A [Tech-A - ITI/10th]). Admin & Allied cadre posts are also filled. Eligibility and syllabus are post-specific. Refer to DRDO/RAC official websites. Pay scales are as per GoI norms.

PSU Recruitment (via GATE/Other Exams)

Many Public Sector Undertakings (BHEL, ONGC, NTPC, GAIL, SAIL, IOCL, HPCL, BPCL, PowerGrid, etc.) recruit Graduate Engineer Trainees (GETs) based on GATE scores in relevant disciplines, followed by Group Discussion and/or Personal Interview. They also conduct separate written exams for other technical (Diploma Engineers, ITI holders) and non-technical (HR, Finance, Marketing - often MBA/CA/relevant PG) roles at various levels. Eligibility for GETs is typically B.E./B.Tech with minimum percentage. Pay scales in PSUs are often attractive (IDA pattern) with good perks.

Teaching Eligibility Tests (CTET, TET, NET, SLET)

These are eligibility tests for teaching positions, not direct recruitment exams for a specific post but a prerequisite for applying to teaching jobs.

  • CTET (Central Teacher Eligibility Test) / TET (State Teacher Eligibility Test): For school teachers (Classes I-V and VI-VIII). Syllabus covers Child Development & Pedagogy, Language I & II, Mathematics, Environmental Studies/Social Studies, Science. Eligibility: D.El.Ed/B.Ed. or equivalent.
  • NET (National Eligibility Test by NTA-UGC): For eligibility for Assistant Professor & Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) in Indian universities and colleges. Syllabus: Paper-I (Teaching/Research Aptitude, Reasoning, Comprehension, DI, ICT, People & Environment, Higher Education System) and Paper-II (Subject-specific at Post Graduate level). Eligibility: Master's Degree with min 55% (relaxations apply).
  • SLET/SET (State Level Eligibility Test): Similar to NET, conducted by states for Assistant Professor eligibility in that state's institutions.
Salary for teachers/professors varies widely based on school/college (Govt/Private), level, and state. Govt. school teachers follow state pay scales. College professors often follow UGC pay scales (e.g., Assistant Professor Pay Level 10 - ₹57,700 basic).
