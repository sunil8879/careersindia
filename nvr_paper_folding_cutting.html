Paper Folding & Cutting

1. Theory: What is Paper Folding & Cutting?

This type of non-verbal reasoning question involves visualizing the outcome of a process. You are shown a transparent sheet of paper being folded one, two, or three times. After the final fold, a cut or a punch is made. You must determine what the paper will look like when it is fully unfolded.

How to Solve: The Unfolding & Mirroring Technique

The most effective method is to work backwards from the final step.

  1. Start with the Cut: Look at the last figure, which shows the folded paper with the cut or punch. This is your starting point.
  2. Unfold Step-by-Step (in Reverse): Unfold the paper in the reverse order of how it was folded. For each step, you need to mirror the cuts.
  3. The Fold Line is a Mirror: The crease from the most recent fold acts as a mirror line. When you unfold the paper, all the existing cuts and holes on one side of the crease are perfectly reflected onto the newly opened side.
  4. Pay Attention to the Axis:
    • If the last fold was horizontal (top to bottom), the unfold will be bottom to top. The fold line is a horizontal mirror.
    • If the last fold was vertical (right to left), the unfold will be left to right. The fold line is a vertical mirror.
    • If the last fold was diagonal, the unfold will mirror the cuts across that diagonal line.
  5. Build the Pattern: With each unfold, you add the mirrored cuts to the existing ones. This gradually builds the complete pattern.
  6. Eliminate Options: After each unfolding step, compare your intermediate result with the answer choices. You can often eliminate one or two incorrect options early on, making the final choice easier.
