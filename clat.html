CLAT – Common Law Admission Test

Eligibility
Candidates must have completed 10+2 (or equivalent) examinations from a recognized board or institution. They should have secured a minimum aggregate score, which may vary from year to year and among different participating universities.
Scope
Admission to 22 National Law Universities (NLUs) (excluding NLU Delhi) and over 50 other law schools.
How to Apply
Online registration via the official CLAT website.
Exam Structure
Five sections: English Language, Current Affairs including General Knowledge, Legal Reasoning, Logical Reasoning, Quantitative Techniques. Total of 120 multiple-choice questions. Duration: 2 hours. Pen-and-paper-based test.
Score Pattern
Each correct answer awards 1 mark; 0.25 marks deducted for each wrong answer.
Fees
Approximately ₹4,000 for General category; ₹3,500 for SC/ST/PwD.
Exam Timing
Conducted once a year, typically in December.
Results
Announced within a few weeks after the exam.
