Simplification: The VBODMAS Rule

Core Concept: The Order of Operations (VBODMAS)

Question

In what order should mathematical operations be performed to correctly simplify an expression?

Answer

The order is defined by the acronym VBODMAS.

Explanation

The speaker breaks down the meaning of each letter, which represents the sequence of operations:

  • V - Vinculum or Bar: Solve the part of the expression that is under a bar first. E.g., in `10 - (8 - 2)`, the `(8 - 2)` part is solved first.
  • B - Bracket: Solve all types of brackets in order: `()` (parentheses), `{}` (curly braces), `[]` (square brackets).
  • O - Of: This represents multiplication, but it has a higher priority than the standard multiplication symbol. E.g., `50 of 2` means `50 * 2`.
  • D - Division (÷): Perform all divisions.
  • M - Multiplication (×): Perform all multiplications.
  • A - Addition (+): Perform all additions.
  • S - Subtraction (-): Perform all subtractions.

Important Notes: The speaker emphasizes, "This order is not optional; it is a strict rule. If you change the order, you will get the wrong answer. The most common mistake is to do addition before division or multiplication. Remember, Division and Multiplication come first, then Addition and Subtraction."

Question 1: Basic BODMAS Application

Question

360 ÷ 36 ÷ 6 ÷ 1 ÷ 5 = ?

Answer

1/3

Explanation

When an expression contains only division, you must solve it from left to right.

  1. 360 ÷ 36 = 10
  2. 10 ÷ 6 = 10/6 = 5/3
  3. 5/3 ÷ 1 = 5/3
  4. 5/3 ÷ 5 = (5/3) * (1/5) = 1/3

Important Notes: The speaker warns, "A lot of students get confused here and try to divide from right to left or in some other order. The rule for operations of the same priority (like all division or all multiplication) is to always proceed from left to right."

Question 2: 'Of' vs. Division

Question

48 ÷ 12 of 2 = ?

Answer

2

Explanation

According to VBODMAS, 'O' (Of) must be solved before 'D' (Division).

  1. First, solve 12 of 2: 12 * 2 = 24.
  2. Now the expression becomes: 48 ÷ 24.
  3. 48 ÷ 24 = 2.

Important Notes: "This is a classic trap," the speaker highlights. "If the question was `48 ÷ 12 * 2`, you would solve from left to right and get `4 * 2 = 8`. But because it says 'of', you MUST solve `12 of 2` first. 'Of' is a special type of multiplication with higher priority than regular division."
