Mirror Images

What is a Mirror Image?

A Mirror Image is a reflection of an object or figure across a mirror. In reasoning problems, this is almost always a vertical mirror. The key principle is lateral inversion, which means the left side of the object appears on the right side in the reflection, and the right side appears on the left.

How to Solve Mirror Image Problems

  • Top and Bottom Remain Unchanged: The most important rule. The top of the original figure will still be at the top in the mirror image, and the bottom will remain at the bottom. This helps eliminate many incorrect options quickly.
  • Left Becomes Right (and Vice-Versa): Imagine a vertical mirror placed next to the figure. Every point on the left of the figure will be reflected to the right, and every point on the right will be reflected to the left.
  • Proximity to the Mirror: The parts of the figure that are closest to the mirror line will be closest to the line in the reflection. Parts that are farthest away will be farthest away in the reflection.
  • Check Orientations: An arrow pointing left (<) will point right (>) in the reflection. An arrow pointing up (^) will still point up (^).
  • Alphanumeric Characters: Be very careful with letters and numbers. Most will be laterally inverted (e.g., 'R' becomes 'Я', '3' becomes 'Ɛ'). Some, like 'A', 'H', 'I', 'M', 'O', 'T', 'U', 'V', 'W', 'X', 'Y', remain the same.
